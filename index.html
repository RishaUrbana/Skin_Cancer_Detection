LEARN ABOUT THE TYPE OF SKIN CANCER

--------WE ARE DETECTING THREE TYPE OF SKIN CANCER-------

NEVUS

A nevus, or mole, is a common skin growth that is usually harmless. However, certain types can develop into melanoma.Regular dermatological check-ups, self-examinations, and sun protection are key preventive measures. Our online skin cancer detection platform helps you easily monitor your moles by analyzing images for early signs of melanoma, promoting timely medical attention and better outcomes.

pigmented benign keratosis

Pigmented benign keratosis, also known as seborrheic keratosis, is a common, non-cancerous skin growth. They are more frequent in older adults and can vary in size and texture. While benign and generally harmless, they can become irritated or itchy.Our online skin cancer detector can help identify if a lesion needs further medical evaluation, ensuring appropriate care and peace of mind.

Melanoma

Melanoma is a serious skin cancer starting in pigment cells. It often appears as a new or changing mole with irregular features. Key signs include asymmetry, irregular borders, varied colors, diameter over 6mm, and evolving shape. Risk factors are UV exposure, fair skin, family history, and numerous moles. Early detection is crucial, and our online tool can help identify suspicious lesions for timely medical attention.
